## Description

At the RE-WORK Deep Learning Summit in 2017, Andrej Karpathy participated in an interview-style session discussing his work at OpenAI. The conversation covered:

- Karpathy's transition from Stanford PhD (where he worked on Recurrent Neural Networks, image captioning, and CS231n) to OpenAI as a research scientist
- The state of deep learning research in 2017, including advancements in reinforcement learning, generative models, and natural language processing
- The emerging paradigm of "Software 2.0" — the idea that neural networks represent a new way of programming where humans specify goals and the network learns the program
- Open challenges in AI safety, interpretability, and generalization
- The future trajectory of AI research and its societal implications
